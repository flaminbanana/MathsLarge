Hilbert's Hotel, a famous thought experiment in mathematics, is a theoretical hotel with infinitely many rooms, each numbered with a positive integer. The paradoxical nature of Hilbert's Hotel lies in its ability to always accommodate new guests, even if all rooms are occupied. This is achieved by shifting the current guests to different rooms, effectively freeing up space for an infinite number of new guests, showcasing the counterintuitive properties of infinite sets.
