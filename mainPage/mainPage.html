Fractals

Fractals are intricate and infinitely complex geometric patterns that repeat at different scales. They're not just shapes; they're a mathematical concept that can be found in nature, art, and even computer-generated graphics. The fascinating thing about fractals is their self-similarity, where each part resembles the whole. Whether in the branching of trees, the coastline of a rugged shore, or the mesmerizing Julia fractal, as seen on the left, fractals offer a glimpse into the beauty of mathematical complexity.


Hilbert's Hotel

Hilbert's Hotel, a famous thought experiment in mathematics, is a theoretical hotel with infinitely many rooms, each numbered with a positive integer. The paradoxical nature of Hilbert's Hotel lies in its ability to always accommodate new guests, even if all rooms are occupied. This is achieved by shifting the current guests to different rooms, effectively freeing up space for an infinite number of new guests, showcasing the counterintuitive properties of infinite sets.
